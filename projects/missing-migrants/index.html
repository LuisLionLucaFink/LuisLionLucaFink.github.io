Mapping the Invisible

Spatial & Temporal Analysis of Death and Missing Along International Migration Routes.

Overview

This dashboard visualizes reported migrant deaths and disappearances worldwide and places them in a spatial and temporal context. It highlights major international migration corridors and applies GIS based analysis to uncover recurring geographic patterns and temporal trends.

Using interactive maps and spatial clustering techniques, the visualizations aim to make high risk regions and migration related mortality patterns more visible and comparable across regions and time periods. The project is intended to support humanitarian awareness and data driven interpretation of migration related risks.

Motivation

Every year, thousands of migrants risk their lives crossing international borders. Many do not survive. While these tragedies are often reduced to numbers, this project uses data to identify where and when deaths and disappearances occur across major migration routes.

By uncovering spatial and temporal patterns in migrant fatalities across the six most dangerous migration corridors, including the Mediterranean, the US Mexico border, the Sahara Desert, and others, the analysis aims to support evidence based action. The goal is not only to visualize loss, but to highlight where preventive measures could save the most lives.

Data Source

The data is provided by the International Organization for Migration through the Missing Migrants Project. It documents deaths and disappearances during migration worldwide. The dataset includes time, location, demographics, and reported cause of death.

It covers the period from 2014 to the present and is continuously updated based on reports from NGOs, media, and official sources.
