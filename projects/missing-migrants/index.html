Mapping the Invisible

Spatial & Temporal Analysis of Death and Missing Along International Migration Routes.

Overview

This dashboard visualizes migrant deaths and disappearances across the globe. It highlights major migration corridors and applies spatial analysis methods to uncover meaningful geographic and temporal patterns.

The visualizations aim to support humanitarian understanding through interactive maps and clustering techniques.

Motivation

Every year, thousands of migrants risk their lives crossing international borders. Many do not survive. While these tragedies are often reduced to numbers, this project uses data to identify where and when these deaths and disappearances occur across multiple major migration routes.

By uncovering spatial and temporal patterns in migrant fatalities across the six most dangerous migration corridors including the Mediterranean, the US-Mexico border and the Sahara Desert, this analysis aims to support evidence-based action.

The goal is not only to visualize loss, but to reveal where preventive measures could save the most lives.

Data Source

The data is provided by the International Organization for Migration through the Missing Migrants Project. It documents deaths and disappearances during migration worldwide.

The dataset includes time, location, demographic information, and cause of death, spanning from 2014 to the present. Data is continuously updated based on reports from NGOs, media sources, and official institutions.

Global Heatmap of Incidents

To provide an initial spatial overview, a global heatmap was created showing where migrant deaths and disappearances have occurred since 2014. Each data point is weighted by the number of dead and missing persons to emphasize severity.

This visualization highlights high-risk zones and geographic concentrations of migrant fatalities and serves as a baseline for more detailed route-specific analyses.

Clear global patterns emerge, with high densities in North Africa, the Central and Eastern Mediterranean, the US-Mexico border region, and the Afghanistan-Iran corridor.
